The collection

Twenty-plus Porsches.
All of them driven.

This is a working collection. Every car in it has been driven. Most have been driven hard.

Members encounter these cars at warehouse events, dinners, and track days throughout the year. They are not behind rope lines or on static display. They are present in the way that a car should be present — available, not protected.

The collection currently sits at 20-plus Porsches spanning four decades of the brand. The list changes. Cars come and go. What stays consistent is the standard: mechanically significant, historically relevant, or built with real intention. Often all three.

2004–2006

Carrera GT

Porsche built 1,270 Carrera GTs between 2004 and 2006. The engine started as a Le Mans racing unit. When the racing program was shelved, Porsche adapted it into a 5.7-liter naturally aspirated V10 and put it in a road car.

The gearbox uses a wood-and-leather shift knob. Not because of tradition — because carbon fiber was too stiff for the mechanical feel the engineers wanted. That specificity runs through every decision in the car.

There is no traction control in the conventional sense. The car was built to communicate, not to correct. Most buyers were not ready for it. The ones who were describe it as the most alive road car they've ever driven.

The founder has owned five across his history with the car. The examples in the collection now are driven.

993 GT2 R

The 993 GT2 R is not a road car with a track package. It is a factory-built competition vehicle, produced for GT class racing in the mid-1990s.

Twin-turbocharged. Rear-wheel drive only — no all-wheel-drive system to soften the delivery. Built by Porsche Motorsport to go racing, which is exactly what it did.

The 993 generation was the last air-cooled 911 Porsche built. This is one of the most significant examples of that generation to exist in a private collection.

DP Motorsport Build

DP Motorsport has been building and converting Porsches since the 1970s. Their work sits in a category that doesn't have a clean label — not a restoration, not a replica, not a restomod. An interpretation, executed by people who have been thinking about 911s for as long as most collectors have been alive.

The DP cars in this collection are not display pieces. They were built to be used and they are used.

Ownership history

Forty-plus cars over time.

The current collection represents where things stand now. Getting here took years and somewhere north of 40 cars.

The significant ones come up in conversation at events: the Carrera GTs from earlier in the decade, the rare 911 variants that moved through before the current lineup took shape, the cars that informed the standard the collection holds today. Not all of them are listed here. That's not the point of this page.

The point is that the cars present in the collection now were chosen the same way everything was chosen: driven, significant, or both. The history behind the collection is what makes that standard credible.

Membership puts you around this collection at events throughout the year.
