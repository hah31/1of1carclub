Why Porsches, specifically.

You can't make another 993 GT2 R. Porsche built fewer than 60 of them and stopped. You can't make another Carrera GT. You can't make another race-winning 935 with Le Mans documentation and a real ownership history.

What you can do is find one, hold it, and wait. The people who did that ten years ago have not been disappointed.

This isn't a thesis about classic cars broadly. It's specific to what the collection holds: cars that were significant when they were built and have become more valuable as the people who understand them have gotten richer and older.

How the ownership structure works here.

Members can hold fractional interest in cars from the collection. The structure is private: no public offering, no ticker, no liquidity promises. Positions are only available to accredited investors who are members in good standing.

What you get is a documented ownership stake in a specific car, held alongside other members who made the same judgment call. The car stays in the collection. You stay in the club. When the car moves, the position moves with it.

The track days and dinners are not separate from this. They're how you know what you own. You drive it. You see it. You understand it. Most asset classes don't give you that.

Who this is built for.

People who have already built capital and are thinking about where to put it next. Who understand that illiquid is not the same as risky. Who want part of their portfolio in something real: a VIN, a history, and a reason to exist beyond a balance sheet.

The membership isn't a purchase. It's a filter. The people in this club are the people you'd want to own something alongside.

What this isn't.

A fund. A startup. A promise.

It's a private club with a real collection and an ownership path that doesn't exist anywhere else. If you want to know more, apply and we'll talk through it directly.
