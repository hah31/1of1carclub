The club

Porsches are built to be driven. The ones that matter most have stories attached to them: racing history, ownership history, mechanical history. The collection behind this club has all of it.

1 of 1 exists for people who want proximity to that. To drive these cars at track days, to spend time around people who've studied them the same way you have, and for the ones who want to go further, to own a piece of them.

What these cars actually are.

The 993 GT2 R was produced in under 60 units. The Carrera GT engine came out of a Le Mans prototype program that Porsche cancelled, then repurposed into a road car that almost nobody could drive. The 935 that won Le Mans in 1979 was run by two brothers from Texas who had no business being there and won anyway.

These aren't just old cars. They're documented objects with racing provenance and shrinking supply. The collector market has treated them accordingly for fifteen years straight.

The people in this club already know this. That's the starting point, not the pitch.

What we're paying attention to.

Air-cooled 911s. Mezger GT cars. Cars that were built before Porsche started designing for comfort. Cars where the driving experience is the whole point, not a feature.

The market for these cars has moved in one direction and it hasn't reversed. That's not hype. It's what happens when you can't make more of something and the people who want it have the money to pay for it.

The club is built around that understanding.

What we're building.

A community across six cities where driving and ownership sit next to each other. Track days and dinners are the social layer. The financial layer is fractional ownership of cars from the collection.

Both are being built the same way: carefully, privately, with people who were already looking for something like this before they found it.

If this is what you've been looking for, apply.
